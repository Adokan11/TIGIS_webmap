TIGIS – Project Information

Overview of processing steps, and methodology of the webmap's results.

1. Project Context

The aim of this project is to identify which historic buildings in Edinburgh’s greenspace would be most suitable to retrofit for community use. Community centres in greenspaces address environmental deprivation by providing accessible social infrastructure where people experience nature, reducing isolation in areas lacking quality environmental amenities. However, their benefits are often overlooked in community and heritage schemes. Therefore, we aim to examine the value of Edinburgh’s greenspaces by assessing their potential to serve as community hubs while preserving and enhancing their historic and cultural significance.

3. Interpreting the Map

The TIGIS webmap combines several spatial layers and interactive tools to help users explore historic sites, nearest green spaces and community centres across Edinburgh. The guidance below explains how to interpret the visualisation and the indicators shown on the map.

3.1 Layers

The map includes five main layers:

  • Sites: Candidate locations for suitability. Each site contains attributes such as proximity to community centres, buffer zone, SIMD rank and nearest green space.
  • Buffers: Circular catchments representing a site’s effective service area. Buffer population counts are used as part of the suitability scoring.
  • Green Spaces: Greenspace polygons classified into several categories (e.g., park, play area, natural space). These are used both as map context and as part of the Greenspace Type criterion.
  • Community Centres: Point locations representing community facilities used in the proximity analysis.
  • SIMD Ranks: SIMD (Scottish Index of Multiple Deprivation) ranks are included to show how deprivation varies across Edinburgh. Lower ranks represent more deprived areas.

3.2 Colour Schemes

Site markers can be coloured according to their final ranking. When the Colour by Rank option is enabled, lower ranking sites appear in darker colours while higher ranking sites appear in lighter tones. This helps users quickly identify the most suitable locations fro our analysis.

3.3 Filters

Several filters allow users to refine which sites are displayed:

  • Final Score Range: Adjust the minimum and maximum AHP score to view only sites within a chosen suitability band.
  • Buffer Population: Limit sites to those serving a minimum or maximum population within their buffer catchment.
  • Community Centre Distance: Show sites that fall within a specific distance range from a community centre.
  • Greenspace Type Filter: Display only sites associated with selected types of greenspaces.

3.4 Searching

The search function allows users to locate specific sites by name or reference code. When a site is selected from the search, the map zooms to its location and displays the full attribute panel for closer inspection.

3.5 Interaction Tools

Clicking on a site opens a detailed information panel showing:

  • Final suitability score and contributing criteria values
  • Population within the buffer zone
  • Distance to nearest community centre
  • Associated greenspace type
